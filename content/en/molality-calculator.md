# Physical Chemistry Guide to Molality, Colligative Properties, and Solution Stoichiometry

In thermodynamics and physical chemistry, **Molality ($m$)** is defined as the amount of solute in moles divided by the mass of pure solvent in kilograms:

$$m = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{m_{\text{solvent (kg)}}}$$

Because mass is invariant under temperature and pressure changes, molality is the gold-standard concentration metric for **Colligative Properties**:

1. **Boiling Point Elevation ($\Delta T_b$):** $\Delta T_b = i \cdot K_b \cdot m$
2. **Freezing Point Depression ($\Delta T_f$):** $\Delta T_f = i \cdot K_f \cdot m$

---

## 1. Molality vs Molarity Comparison Matrix

| Property | Molality ($m$) | Molarity ($M$) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | Moles solute per kg solvent | Moles solute per Liter solution |
| **Formula** | $m = n / \text{kg}_{\text{solvent}}$ | $M = n / V_{\text{solution}}$ |
| **Denominator** | Mass of pure solvent (kg) | Total volume of solution (L) |
| **Temperature Effect** | **100% Temperature Independent** | Shifts with liquid volume expansion |
| **Primary Field** | Physical Chemistry & Thermodynamics | Analytical Chemistry & Reagents |

## 3. Van't Hoff Factors ($i$) for Electrolytes & Non-Electrolytes

```
   Glucose / Sucrose (Non-electrolyte): i = 1
   NaCl → Na⁺ + Cl⁻: i = 2
   CaCl₂ → Ca²⁺ + 2 Cl⁻: i = 3
   Al(NO₃)₃ → Al³⁺ + 3 NO₃⁻: i = 4
   Al₂(SO₄)₃ → 2 Al³⁺ + 3 SO₄²⁻: i = 5
```

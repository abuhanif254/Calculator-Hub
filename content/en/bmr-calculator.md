## What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?
Your **Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)** represents the minimum number of calories your body requires to perform the most basic, life-sustaining functions if you were to rest in bed for 24 hours. These critical functions include breathing, blood circulation, cellular growth, brain function, and regulating body temperature.

For most individuals, your BMR accounts for roughly **60% to 75%** of your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). Note that BMR does *not* include the calories you burn from walking, exercising, or even digesting food.

Our **Online BMR Calculator** utilizes the **Mifflin-St Jeor equation**, widely recognized by health professionals and dieticians as the most accurate clinical formula for estimating resting metabolism.

### The Science
The core biological parameters that determine your BMR are:
*   **Weight & Height:** Larger bodies require more energy (calories) to sustain cellular volume and tissue maintenance.
*   **Age:** BMR naturally decreases as you age—typically dropping by 1-2% per decade after the age of 20, largely due to age-related muscle loss (sarcopenia).
*   **Gender / Genetics:** Men generally possess less body fat and higher muscle mass than women of the same age and weight, resulting in a naturally higher basal metabolic rate.

### How to Apply Your BMR
Knowing your basal metabolic rate is the absolute first step in nutritional planning:
1.  **Calculate BMR:** Find your baseline (e.g., 1,600 calories).
2.  **Apply Activity Multipliers:** Multiply your BMR by your physical activity level to find your TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure, available in our dedicated Calorie Calculator).
3.  **Adjust for Goals:** Never eat strictly *below* your BMR for extended periods without medical supervision, as this can severely disrupt metabolic hormones and crash your energy levels.

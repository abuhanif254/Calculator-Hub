# Stoichiometry & Physical Chemistry Guide to the Mole Concept and Avogadro's Number

In chemistry, the **Mole ($n$)** serves as the universal conversion hub bridging macroscopic laboratory measurements (mass in grams, volume in Liters) with microscopic chemical entities (atoms, molecules, formula units, and ions).

$$\text{Mass (g)} \xrightarrow{\quad \div M \quad} \text{Moles (n)} \xrightarrow{\quad \times N_A \quad} \text{Particles (N)}$$

---

## 1. Fundamental Mole Relationships

1. **Mass-to-Mole Equation:**
   $$n = \frac{m}{M_{\text{g/mol}}}$$
2. **Mole-to-Particle Equation (Avogadro's Constant $N_A = 6.02214076 \times 10^{23}$):**
   $$N = n \cdot N_A$$
3. **Mass of a Single Particle ($m_{\text{particle}}$):**
   $$m_{\text{particle}} = \frac{M_{\text{g/mol}}}{N_A}$$

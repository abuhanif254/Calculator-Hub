## What is a Pregnancy Calculator?
Whether you're actively trying to conceive or have just discovered you're expecting, knowing your **Estimated Due Date (EDD)** is the single most important biological milestone for tracking your baby's development and preparing for childbirth.

Our **Online Pregnancy Calculator** computes your exact developmental timeline and expected delivery timeframe using mathematically proven OB/GYN clinical methodologies.

### Calculation Methods
Because pregnancies occur and are tracked via different clinical timelines, our engine supports the three primary medical frameworks for predicting childbirth:

1.  **First Day of Last Period (LMP):** This is the most common clinical method. By default, a human pregnancy lasts roughly 280 days (40 weeks) from the first day of your last menstrual period. Our dynamic engine also adjusts the output if your personal cycle length is longer or shorter than the standard 28-day average.
2.  **Conception Date:** If you tracked your ovulation closely or know the exact date of conception, this method bypasses the LMP estimation and mathematically adds exactly 266 days (38 weeks) from fertilization to determine the delivery timeline.
3.  **IVF Transfer Date:** For in-vitro fertilization, gestational age is mathematically exact. The calculation identifies the precise transfer date and compensates for the exact cellular age of the embryo (typically a 3-day or 5-day transfer).

### Understanding Trimesters
Human gestation spans three distinct biological developmental phases called trimesters:
*   **First Trimester (Weeks 1 – 13):** Organogenesis. The most critical period of structural development.
*   **Second Trimester (Weeks 14 – 27):** Often called the "golden period." Rapid systemic growth and the beginning of fetal movement (quickening).
*   **Third Trimester (Weeks 28 – 40+):** Massive weight gain and final lung/brain maturity in preparation for exiting the womb.

# Comprehensive Guide to Mass Density, Specific Gravity, and Buoyancy Physics

Density ($\rho$) is a fundamental intensive property of matter that quantifies how tightly mass is packed into a given spatial volume.

Whether calculating structural dead loads for reinforced concrete bridges, verifying gold purity in jewelry, designing naval ship hulls, or predicting whether a material will float or sink in water, mastering $\rho = \frac{m}{V}$ and Archimedes' Principle is essential.

---

## 1. Defining Mass Density ($\rho = \frac{m}{V}$)

Mass density ($\rho$, the Greek letter *rho*) is defined as mass per unit volume:

$$\rho = \frac{m}{V}$$

Where:
* $\rho$ is Density in kilograms per cubic meter ($\text{kg/m}^3$).
* $m$ is Mass in kilograms ($\text{kg}$).
* $V$ is Volume in cubic meters ($\text{m}^3$).

---

## 2. Specific Gravity ($\text{SG}$)

Specific Gravity ($\text{SG}$) is a dimensionless ratio comparing a material's density to the density of pure water at $4^\circ\text{C}$ ($\rho_{water} = 1,000\text{ kg/m}^3$):

$$\text{SG} = \frac{\rho_{material}}{\rho_{water}}$$

* **$\text{SG} < 1.0$:** Material is less dense than water and will float.
* **$\text{SG} = 1.0$:** Material has neutral buoyancy in water.
* **$\text{SG} > 1.0$:** Material is denser than water and will sink.

---

## 3. Archimedes' Principle & Buoyancy

Archimedes' Principle states that a body immersed in a fluid experiences an upward buoyant force ($F_B$) equal to the weight of the fluid it displaces:

$$F_B = \rho_{fluid} \cdot V_{submerged} \cdot g$$

```
Object Density vs Fluid Density:
├── ρ_object < ρ_fluid  ==>  Floats at Surface (Buoyant Force > Weight)
├── ρ_object = ρ_fluid  ==>  Neutrally Buoyant (Suspended in Mid-water)
└── ρ_object > ρ_fluid  ==>  Sinks to Bottom (Weight > Buoyant Force)
```

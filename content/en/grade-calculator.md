## Understanding Grading Systems

Before you can calculate your final grade, you must look at your syllabus and determine which of the two standard grading systems your professor is using. 

### 1. The Point-Based System (Unweighted)
This is the simplest grading system. The professor assigns a specific, static point value to every single assignment in the class. 
*   Homework 1: 10 points
*   Quiz 1: 20 points
*   Midterm: 100 points
*   Final Exam: 200 points
*   **Total Points Possible:** 330 points.

To find your grade, you simply take the total number of points you have earned and divide it by the total number of points possible. If you earned 280 points out of 330, your grade is an 84.8% (B). 

### 2. The Weighted Category System
This is the most common system in higher education, and it is mathematically much more complex. Instead of raw points, the professor assigns a percentage "weight" to entire categories of work. 
*   Homework: 15% of your total grade
*   Quizzes: 20% of your total grade
*   Midterm Exam: 25% of your total grade
*   Final Exam: 40% of your total grade

In this system, getting a 100% on a homework assignment barely affects your final grade, while getting a 60% on the heavily weighted Final Exam can destroy your entire semester's work.

Our free online Grade Calculator is specifically designed to handle complex, weighted grading systems. It serves two distinct purposes: calculating your current standing, and predicting what you need on your final exam.

### Mode 1: Find Your Current Grade
To determine exactly where you stand right now, you must input the grades you have already received.

2.  **Grade Received:** The percentage you earned (e.g., 85%).
3.  **Weight:** The percentage this assessment is worth according to the syllabus (e.g., 20%).

1.  Input all your current grades and their weights.

The engine will instantly output the exact minimum percentage you must score on the final exam. If it says you need an 88.5%, you know exactly how hard you need to study. If it says you need a 112%, it is mathematically impossible to get an 'A', and you can adjust your expectations and study time accordingly.

## The Myth of Extra Credit

Many students assume that extra credit is the magic solution to a failing grade. In a weighted system, this is often a mathematical illusion. 

If your professor offers a 10-point extra credit assignment, but it goes into the "Homework" category which is only weighted at 10% of your total grade, those 10 points are severely diluted. They might only raise your final grade by 0.2%. 

Conversely, if a professor curves a Midterm Exam (weighted at 30%) by 5 points, that curve has massive mathematical leverage and can easily shift your final grade by an entire letter. Always use the calculator to determine the actual value of extra credit before you spend hours doing it.

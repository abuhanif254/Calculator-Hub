# Comprehensive Guide to Electrical Power, AC Power Triangles, and Energy Costing

Electrical power ($P$, measured in Watts) measures the instantaneous rate of electrical energy transfer, conversion, or dissipation in a circuit.

Whether calculating DC wattage, solving 1-phase and 3-phase AC power triangles ($P, Q, S$), assessing Power Factor ($\text{PF} = \cos\phi$), estimating residential electric bills, or sizing solar inverters, mastering $P = V \cdot I \cdot \text{PF}$ and $S = \sqrt{P^2 + Q^2}$ is vital to modern electrical engineering.

---

## 1. Defining Electrical Power ($P = \frac{E}{t}$)

In fundamental physics, **electrical power** ($P$) is defined as energy ($E$, Joules) per unit time ($t$, seconds):

$$P = \frac{E}{t} = V \cdot I$$

$$1\text{ Watt } (W) = 1\frac{\text{Joule}}{\text{Second}} = 1\text{ Volt} \times 1\text{ Ampere}$$

---

## 2. Real, Apparent, and Reactive Power Formulas

```
The Power Triangle:
         S = Apparent Power (VA) [Hypotenuse]
         P = Real Power (W)      [Horizontal Leg]
         Q = Reactive Power (VAR)[Vertical Leg]
         PF = P / S = cos(φ)
```

1. **DC Power:**
   $$P = V \cdot I = \frac{V^2}{R} = I^2 \cdot R$$
2. **Single-Phase AC Real Power:**
   $$P = V \cdot I \cdot \text{PF}\quad (\text{Watts})$$
3. **Single-Phase AC Apparent Power:**
   $$S = V \cdot I\quad (\text{VA})$$
4. **Single-Phase AC Reactive Power:**
   $$Q = \sqrt{S^2 - P^2} = V \cdot I \cdot \sin\phi\quad (\text{VAR})$$
5. **Three-Phase AC Real Power:**
   $$P = \sqrt{3} \cdot V_L \cdot I_L \cdot \text{PF}\quad (\text{Watts})$$
6. **Three-Phase AC Apparent Power:**
   $$S = \sqrt{3} \cdot V_L \cdot I_L\quad (\text{VA})$$

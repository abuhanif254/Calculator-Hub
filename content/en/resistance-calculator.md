# Comprehensive Guide to Electrical Resistance, Resistor Color Codes, and Wire Calculations

Electrical resistance ($R$, measured in Ohms $\Omega$) measures a component's fundamental opposition to electric current flow.

Whether decoding 4-band resistor color codes, constructing series and parallel resistor networks, sizing wire conductors, or calculating heat dissipation ($P = I^2 R$), mastering $R = \frac{V}{I}$ and $R = \rho \frac{L}{A}$ is vital to modern physics and electronics.

---

## 1. Defining Electrical Resistance ($R = \frac{V}{I}$)

According to **Ohm's Law**, electrical resistance ($R$) is the ratio of applied electric potential difference ($V$) to current flow ($I$):

$$R = \frac{V}{I}$$

$$1\text{ Ohm } (\Omega) = 1\frac{\text{Volt}}{\text{Ampere}}$$

---

## 2. Ohm's & Power Law Equations for Resistance

```
Resistance Formula Triangle:
         R = V / I
         R = P / I²
         R = V² / P
```

1. **From Voltage & Current:**
   $$R = \frac{V}{I}$$
2. **From Power & Current:**
   $$R = \frac{P}{I^2}$$
3. **From Voltage & Power:**
   $$R = \frac{V^2}{P}$$

## Understanding House Affordability

Buying a house is a monumental financial decision. Before looking at homes and applying for mortgages, it is critical to determine exactly **how much house you can afford**. Our House Affordability Calculator looks at your financial profile through the eyes of a mortgage lender to estimate your maximum purchasing power.

### How Do Lenders Determine Affordability?

Lenders evaluate your ability to repay a loan using a metric called the **Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio**. This ratio compares your total monthly debt payments against your gross monthly income. 

* **Front-End Ratio:** The percentage of your income that goes toward housing costs (mortgage principal, interest, property taxes, and homeowners insurance). Lenders typically want this to be no higher than 28%.
* **Back-End Ratio:** The percentage of your income that goes toward *all* recurring debt (housing costs + car loans + student loans + credit cards). This is usually capped at 36%, though some loan programs may allow up to 43% or even 50% under strict conditions.

### Variables That Shape Your Budget

1.  **Gross Income:** This is your total household income *before* taxes and deductions. It serves as the baseline for the entire calculation.
2.  **Existing Monthly Debt:** High existing debt payments dramatically reduce the amount of mortgage you can take on. Paying off a car loan or credit card can massively boost your home buying budget.
3.  **Down Payment:** The larger your down payment, the larger the total home price you can afford. A bigger down payment also helps you secure better interest rates and avoid costly Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI).
4.  **Interest Rates:** Even a 0.5% shift in interest rates can change your buying power by tens of thousands of dollars.

### The Hidden Costs of Homeownership

Your affordable mortgage payment isn't just principal and interest (P&I). It is actually **PITI**:
*   **P**rincipal
*   **I**nterest
*   **T**axes (Property Taxes)
*   **I**nsurance (Homeowners Insurance)

In addition, if you are buying a condo or moving into a planned community, you must account for **HOA fees**. Our calculator incorporates all these variables to give you a realistic and safe budget.

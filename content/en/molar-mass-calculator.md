# Analytical & Physical Chemistry Guide to Molar Mass, Molecular Weight, and Percentage Composition

Calculating **Molar Mass ($M$)** is the fundamental first step in stoichiometry, solution preparation, chemical thermodynamics, and analytical mass spectrometry:

$$M = \sum_{i=1}^{k} N_i \cdot \text{AW}_i$$

---

## 1. Molar Mass vs Molecular Mass vs Formula Mass Matrix

| Metric | Symbol / Unit | Applies To | Definition |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Molar Mass** | $M$ ($\text{g/mol}$) | All Chemical Substances | Mass of 1 mole ($6.022 \times 10^{23}$ entities) in grams |
| **Molecular Mass** | $m_{\text{molecule}}$ ($\text{u}$ / $\text{Da}$) | Covalent Compounds | Mass of 1 single covalent molecule |
| **Formula Mass** | $m_{\text{formula}}$ ($\text{u}$ / $\text{Da}$) | Ionic Crystal Lattices | Mass of 1 empirical formula unit (e.g. $\text{NaCl}$) |

## 3. Hydrate Water Breakdown Protocol

```
   Step 1: Calculate Molar Mass of Anhydrous Salt (M_anhydrous).
   Step 2: Multiply water molecule count (x) by Molar Mass of H2O (18.015 g/mol).
   Step 3: Sum Total Molar Mass = M_anhydrous + (x × 18.015).
   Step 4: % Water by Mass = [ (x × 18.015) / M_total ] × 100%.
```

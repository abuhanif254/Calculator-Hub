## Modeling Your Financial Future
Investing is one of the most reliable ways to build long-term wealth, but understanding exactly *how* that wealth will materialize can be difficult to conceptualize. Our Investment Calculator uses the mathematical principles of future value and compound growth to project what your current assets and ongoing contributions will be worth decades from now.

### The Dynamics of Compound Growth
The cornerstone of any investment calculation is **Compound Interest**. Unlike simple interest (which only pays a percentage on your initial deposit), compound interest pays you returns on both your initial deposit *and* all the accumulated interest from previous years. Formally, this creates an exponential growth curve.

**Why Contributions Matter**
While starting with a large initial sum is fantastic, continuous ongoing contributions are the true engine of most retail investment portfolios. By adding capital every single month (or year), you are continually increasing the base upon which those compounding returns are calculated. This helps smooth out market volatility through a process called "Dollar Cost Averaging."

### Using the Calculator
To generate a projection, you'll need to define a few key parameters:
1.  **Starting Amount:** This is the money you are initially putting down (your Phase 0 capital base).
2.  **Additional Contribution:** The exact amount of money you plan to add periodically to the investment account.
3.  **Contribution Frequency:** Whether you are adding that extra money every *Month* or once a *Year*. (Monthly contributions generally result in slightly higher returns due to continuous intra-year compounding).
4.  **Expected Return Rate:** The average percentage you expect the investment to grow annually. Over the last 50 years, total stock market indexes have historically hovered around 7-10% prior to inflation.
5.  **Years to Grow:** Your timeline. The longer the timeline, the more devastatingly effective compounding becomes.

## Maximize Your Tax-Free Retirement Wealth

Planning for retirement is one of the most important financial steps you can take. Our **Roth IRA Calculator** is designed to help you visualize your future wealth, providing dynamic pie charts and detailed breakdowns of your contributions versus the compound interest you earn.

### Why Choose a Roth IRA?

Unlike traditional IRAs or 401(k)s where your money is taxed upon withdrawal, a Roth IRA provides **tax-free growth**. This means that if you invest $100,000 over your lifetime and it grows to $1,000,000, that entire $900,000 of growth is completely yours—free from federal income taxes. 

### Key Features of a Roth IRA

1. **Tax-Free Withdrawals:** Since you already paid taxes on the money you contributed, qualified withdrawals in retirement are 100% tax-free.
2. **No Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs):** Unlike traditional accounts, a Roth IRA doesn't force you to withdraw your money at age 73. You can leave it to grow for your entire lifetime and pass it on to heirs.
3. **Withdraw Contributions Penalty-Free:** In an emergency, you can withdraw your *direct contributions* (but not the earnings) at any time, penalty-free and tax-free.

### How to Use the Roth IRA Calculator

To get the most accurate projection of your retirement savings, simply enter:
- **Current Age:** Your age today.
- **Expected Retirement Age:** The age you plan to stop working and start withdrawing funds. (Usually between 60 and 67).
- **Current Roth IRA Balance:** The amount you have already invested in your Roth IRA.
- **Annual Contribution:** How much you plan to invest every year.
- **Expected Annual Rate of Return:** A realistic expectation of stock market growth. (7% is a common baseline adjusted for inflation).

### Understanding the Results

Our dynamic pie chart will instantly calculate and display your **Total Balance at Retirement**. More importantly, it divides your final balance into two categories:
- **Total Contributions:** The actual hard-earned cash you deposited into the account.
- **Total Earned Growth (Interest):** The power of compound interest. In most long-term scenarios, this growth will drastically exceed your total contributions!

### Global Equivalents for International Visitors

While the Roth IRA is specific to the United States, the concept of a tax-advantaged, after-tax investment account exists globally. If you are an international visitor, you can use our calculator to model equivalents such as:
- **United Kingdom:** Individual Savings Account (ISA)
- **Canada:** Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA)
- **Australia:** Superannuation (Non-Concessional Contributions)

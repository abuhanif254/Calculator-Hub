# Comprehensive Guide to Mass, Weight Force, and Planetary Gravity Physics

Weight ($W$) is the gravitational pull force acting on a physical body of mass ($m$) within a gravitational acceleration field ($g$).

Whether calculating structural foundation loads, sizing aerospace rocket engines for Earth liftoff, analyzing fighter jet G-forces, or determining how much a person weighs on the Moon, Mars, or Jupiter, mastering $W = m \cdot g$ is fundamental to physics education and mechanical engineering.

---

## 1. Defining Weight Force ($W = m \cdot g$)

In Newtonian mechanics, **weight** ($W$) is defined as the product of invariant mass ($m$) and local gravitational acceleration ($g$):

$$W = m \cdot g$$

Where:
* $W$ is Weight in Newtons ($\text{N} = \text{kg}\cdot\text{m}/\text{s}^2$).
* $m$ is Mass in kilograms ($\text{kg}$).
* $g$ is Acceleration due to gravity in meters per second squared ($\text{m/s}^2$).

---

```
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                              MASS (m)                                 |
| - Intrinsic scalar property measuring matter quantity                 |
| - CONSTANT everywhere (Earth, Moon, Mars, Deep Space)                 |
| - SI Unit: Kilogram (kg)                                              |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
                                   │
                                   ▼ (Multiplied by local Gravity 'g')
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                             WEIGHT (W)                                |
| - Downward vector force exerted by gravitational field                |
| - VARIABLE depending on celestial body location                      |
| - SI Unit: Newton (N)                                                 |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

## 4. Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation Derivation

Surface gravity $g$ of any spherical body is derived from Newton's Universal Gravitation Law:

$$F = \frac{G \cdot M \cdot m}{R^2} \implies g = \frac{G \cdot M}{R^2}$$

Where:
* $G = 6.67430 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$ (Gravitational constant).
* $M$ is planet mass in kilograms.
* $R$ is planet radius in meters.

## What is a Social Security Calculator?
A **Social Security Calculator** is a critical retirement planning tool that estimates your future monthly Social Security benefits. By inputting your current age, annual income, and intended retirement age, you can forecast your monthly income during retirement.

Our calculator uses a simplified model based on standard government formulas (AIME and PIA) to give you an immediate, easy-to-understand estimate.

### How Does Retirement Age Affect Your Benefits?
Your monthly benefit amount is heavily influenced by the age at which you choose to start claiming your Social Security:
1.  **Early Retirement (Age 62):** You can start claiming benefits as early as age 62, but your monthly payout will be permanently reduced by up to 30%.
2.  **Full Retirement Age (FRA - typically 67):** This is the age at which you are entitled to 100% of your primary insurance amount (PIA). For anyone born in 1960 or later, the FRA is 67.
3.  **Delayed Retirement (Up to Age 70):** If you wait past your FRA to claim benefits, your monthly payout increases by approximately 8% for every year you wait, up until age 70.

### Why You Need This Estimate
Knowing your projected Social Security income is the foundation of retirement planning. Since Social Security is rarely enough to cover all expenses on its own, this estimate helps you determine how much additional money you need to save in your 401(k), IRA, or other investment accounts to maintain your lifestyle.

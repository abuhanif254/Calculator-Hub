## Understanding Salary Conversions
Negotiating a new job offer or budgeting for a new expense requires knowing exactly how much money is coming in at different intervals. Finding out your hourly rate from an annual salary, or mapping an hourly gig onto a year-long projection, can be difficult to calculate manually.

Our **Salary Conversion Calculator** breaks down your base wage instantly across the entire calendar.

### The Conversion Mechanics
By adjusting the baseline assumptions (such as standard working hours and working days), you can see exactly how a single number unfolds over a full 52-week calendar year:
*   **Hourly Rate:** The exact amount paid for 60 minutes of labor.
*   **Daily Pay:** The hourly rate multiplied by the hours worked per day.
*   **Weekly Pay:** The weekly average, which often forms the baseline for budgeting groceries or weekly rent.
*   **Monthly Pay:** Annual salary divided evenly by 12.
*   **Annual Salary:** The total gross value of a full 52-week working calendar.

### Why You Need This
Employers often pitch salaries in whatever format sounds the most appealing (e.g., "Make $30 an hour!" or "Earn $60k a year!"). To make an apples-to-apples comparison between job offers, or to figure out if your hourly side-hustle can replace your annual full-time salary, you must standardize the numbers. This calculator standardizes them instantly.

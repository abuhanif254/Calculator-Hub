## The Complete Guide to Auto Leasing

Determining whether an auto lease is a good deal requires looking past just the monthly payment. Dealerships often structure leases to hide the true cost of financing and depreciation. Our free **Lease Calculator** breaks down every element of a car lease so that you understand exactly what you are paying for over the term.

### The Mechanics of a Car Lease

When you buy a car, you are paying for the entire value of the vehicle. When you lease, you are only paying for the **depreciation** that occurs while you drive it, plus a finance charge and taxes. 

Understanding the "big three" variables of leasing is the key to negotiating a better deal:

**1. Capitalized (Cap) Cost:**
Think of this as the purchase price. Negotiate this number exactly as if you were buying the car for cash. A lower cap cost directly reduces the amount of depreciation you must pay for. 

**2. Residual Value:**
This is predetermined by the leasing company and represents what the car will be worth at the end of the lease. A higher residual value means less depreciation, which results in a lower monthly payment.

**3. Money Factor:**
This is the interest rate on the lease. It's normally presented as a small decimal (e.g., 0.00125). Multiply it by 2400 to get the APR (in this case, 3.0%). This is highly negotiable based on your credit score.

### Breaking Down Your Monthly Payment

The interactive chart output by our calculator shows your monthly payment split into three distinct categories:
- **Depreciation:** The core of the lease—the vehicle's drop in value.
- **Finance Charge:** The rent charge for borrowing the leasing company's money.
- **Taxes:** The applicable local sales tax applied to your monthly payment.

### Why Down Payments Are Risky on a Lease

It might be tempting to put $5,000 down on a lease to secure a $250 monthly payment. However, if that vehicle is totaled or stolen three weeks later, your insurance company reimburses the leasing company for the vehicle's value, but your $5,000 down payment is almost never recovered. It is generally advised to put zero down on a lease and roll the costs into the monthly payments.

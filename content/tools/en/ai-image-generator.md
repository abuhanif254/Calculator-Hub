### How Diffusion Models Work

To truly master AI image generation, it helps to understand the underlying technology. Modern AI generators (like DALL-E 3, Midjourney, Imagen, and Stable Diffusion) use a machine learning architecture called **Diffusion**.

1.  **Training Phase:** The AI model is trained on billions of image-text pairs. It learns the visual concepts of objects, textures, lighting, and artistic styles by associating them with human words.
2.  **Adding Noise:** During training, the model takes a clear image and progressively adds "Gaussian noise" (static) until the image is completely unrecognizable. 
3.  **The Denoising Process:** The AI is then trained to reverse this process. It learns how to take pure static noise and carefully subtract it step-by-step to recover the original image.
4.  **Text-Guided Generation:** When you input a text prompt into our tool, the AI starts with a canvas of pure random noise. Guided by your text, it systematically "denoises" the canvas, sculpting the static into a coherent image that matches your description. 

This process is why every generated image is fundamentally unique. It is not copying and pasting existing images; it is mathematically dreaming up a new visual reality from scratch.

### Mastering Prompt Engineering

The quality of your output is directly proportional to the quality of your input. Crafting the perfect text description is known as **Prompt Engineering**.

A strong prompt typically follows a specific structure:
*   **Subject:** What is the main focus? (e.g., "A futuristic cyberpunk city", "A cute golden retriever").
*   **Medium/Style:** How should it look? (e.g., "Oil painting", "3D render in Unreal Engine 5", "Photorealistic portrait", "Vector flat illustration").
*   **Lighting:** How is the scene illuminated? (e.g., "Cinematic lighting", "Golden hour", "Neon glow", "Soft studio lighting").
*   **Environment/Context:** Where is this happening? (e.g., "In a dense rainy forest", "On Mars", "Against a pure white background").
*   **Camera/Lens (for realism):** (e.g., "Shot on 35mm lens", "Macro photography", "Depth of field", "Drone shot").

**Example of a weak prompt:** "A cat in space."
**Example of a strong prompt:** "A highly detailed, photorealistic portrait of an orange tabby cat wearing a futuristic spacesuit, floating inside an illuminated spaceship cabin, cinematic lighting, 8k resolution, hyper-detailed."

### Advanced Generation Parameters Explained

Our generator provides advanced settings to give you granular control over the AI's output:

#### Negative Prompts
While the main prompt tells the AI what you *want*, the Negative Prompt tells it what to *avoid*. If you are generating a portrait and notice the AI keeps adding glasses, you can type "glasses, spectacles" into the negative prompt. Standard negative prompts often include: "blurry, mutated, extra fingers, ugly, watermark, text."

#### Aspect Ratio
Different projects require different dimensions. Our tool lets you instantly define the canvas shape:
*   **1:1 (Square):** Ideal for Instagram posts and profile pictures.
*   **16:9 (Landscape):** Perfect for YouTube thumbnails, blog headers, and desktop wallpapers.
*   **9:16 (Portrait):** The standard for TikTok, Instagram Reels, and mobile phone wallpapers.

Once generated, if you need to physically crop the image further, use our [Crop Image](/en/tools/crop-image) tool.

#### Guidance Scale (CFG Scale)
The Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG) scale dictates how strictly the AI should adhere to your text prompt. 
*   **Low Scale (e.g., 3-5):** The AI is more creative and takes artistic liberties. The result might deviate from your prompt but could be visually stunning.
*   **High Scale (e.g., 10-15):** The AI strictly follows your prompt. This is useful for precise instructions but can sometimes result in artifacts or "deep-fried" looking images if pushed too high.

#### Seed
Every time the AI starts with random noise, it uses a specific numerical "Seed". By default, the seed is random, which is why generating the same prompt twice yields different results. If you generate an image you love and want to make only a tiny tweak to the prompt, you can lock the specific Seed number. This forces the AI to use the exact same starting noise, resulting in a very similar composition.

### Commercial Use, Copyright, and Ethics

A frequent question surrounding AI art is: "Who owns it, and can I use it for business?"

In most jurisdictions (including the US Copyright Office rulings), AI-generated images that are purely the result of a text prompt cannot be copyrighted by the user, because a human did not physically "author" the pixels. This means the images are effectively in the **Public Domain**. 

**Can you use them commercially?** Yes. You can use the images generated by our tool for your blog, marketing campaigns, video games, or merchandise. However, because they are in the public domain, you cannot sue someone else for downloading and using the image you generated. 

*Disclaimer: Always ensure your prompts do not infringe on existing trademarks (e.g., generating Mickey Mouse) or the likeness of real living people, as this can lead to trademark or right-of-publicity violations, independent of copyright law.*
